# Launch Strategy

Use this rule to plan and execute product launches, feature announcements, and go-to-market strategies that build compounding momentum.

## 1. The ORB Framework (Marketing Channels)
- **Owned**: Channels you control (Email list, Blog, Podcast, Community). These provide direct access and compound value.
- **Rented**: Platforms with algorithms (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Reddit, App Stores). Use these to drive traffic to *Owned* channels.
- **Borrowed**: Tapping into others' audiences (Guest content, Collaborations, Influencers, Podcasts). Use for instant credibility and reach.

## 2. Five-Phase Launch Approach
1. **Internal**: Friendly users, usability testing, iron out major issues.
2. **Alpha**: External validation, landing page with waitlist, individual invites.
3. **Beta**: Scaled access, teasers, influencer testing, refined messaging.
4. **Early Access**: Expansion (batches or open), usage data collection, PMF surveys.
5. **Full Launch**: Open self-serve, GA announcement, maximum visibility across all channels.

## 3. Product Hunt Strategy
- **Preparation**: Build relationships, optimize listing (compelling tagline, polished visuals, demo video).
- **Execution**: All-day engagement, respond to every comment in real-time.
- **Conversion**: Direct traffic back to owned channels (email signups).

## 4. Post-Launch Momentum
- **Educate**: Automated onboarding sequences.
- **Reinforce**: Roundup emails and newsletters.
- **Differentiate**: Comparison pages and interactive demos.
- **Signals**: Regular changelog updates to show active development.

## 5. Launch Checklist
- [ ] Landing page & Email capture ready.
- [ ] Owned/Rented/Borrowed channels identified.
- [ ] Launch assets (GIFs, screenshots, video) created.
- [ ] Onboarding flow & Tracking in place.
- [ ] Multi-channel announcement schedule defined.

> **Philosophy**: Don't just launch once. Every improvement is an opportunity to capture attention.
